About the program

A summer course built around real agrifood challenges

Held every July–August, SAMI brings together students and young professionals from across the world to study how climate resilience, ESG practice, and sustainable agrifood systems intersect — through live online sessions, a hybrid on-campus week, and a guided site visit.

The program runs in two connected formats. Everyone joins twelve live online courses covering ESG fundamentals, supply chain sustainability, and climate-smart agriculture, taught by academics and practitioners from Indonesia, Europe, and Asia Pacific. Participants who choose the extended track then travel to IPB University in Bogor and on to Garut for two hybrid courses and four days of field learning.

Alongside the coursework, an essay competition sharpens participants' critical thinking on agrifood sustainability, with results announced at the closing ceremony in Bogor.
